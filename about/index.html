About Me

Hello!

You have gotten to this page for a reason, and that reason is to learn more about me, so here goes:

I am Arseniy Kouzmenkov, a third year Computing Science Honors student at the University of Alberta.

For developing, I prefer to use C++ and C for larger projects, Java for Android development and Python for small scripts. I really enjoy system development and working with low level networking. The Operating Systems and Computer Organization and Architecture classes have been some of my favourite courses so far!

One of my non-programming interests is teaching. After all, when you teach others, you solidify your own knowledge. The feeling when a person you helped does well with the knowledge you gave them is simply exhilarating. Because of this passion, I became a Teaching Assistant for CMPUT 274 and CMPUT 275 in the 2018 / 2019 academic year at the UofA. I spent many hours assisting students with course content and helping them further their undestanding of what it means to be a great developer. In addition to that, during my internship with the Worker’s Compensation Board of Alberta, I spent a week running training sessions on the topic of Test Automation.

As for hobbies, I love most aquatic sports: I did competitive swimming for seven years and have Open Water PADI certification for scuba diving. Feel free to ask me about my favourite dive sites!
